About Me

Hello! I am a scientist and engineer with 5 years of experience developing neural cell therapies for chronic neurological disorders. My work spans computational and experimental biology, and I thrive working on a team of scientists with diverse skill sets to tackle pressing medical challenges.

I’m currently finishing up my last semester of an MS in Bioinformatics at Brandeis University while working as a process engineer at Neurona Therapeutics.

Previously, I graduated from the Whiting School of Engineering at Johns Hopkins University with a concurrent BS/MSE in chemical and biomolecular engineering. My thesis was on a novel combination therapy for cancer treatment using multicomponent drug amphiphiles, and my general focus was on molecular and cellular bioengineering.
